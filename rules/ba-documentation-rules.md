# BA Documentation Rules

These rules define how BA-kit documents are structured, named, and maintained.

Related rules:
- [BA Primary Workflow](./ba-primary-workflow.md)
- [BA Quality Standards](./ba-quality-standards.md)

## Core Rules
- Use the template library in `../../templates/` whenever a template exists.
- Keep document titles, headings, and filenames aligned.
- Prefer markdown-first deliverables with clear sections and stable anchors.
- Preserve traceability links between source, analysis, and final outputs.

## Naming
- Use descriptive kebab-case filenames.
- Keep final artifacts in `docs/` and working outputs in `plans/` or `plans/reports/`.
- Avoid ambiguous names like `final.md` or `notes.md`.

## Versioning
- Major version for scope or structure changes.
- Minor version for content updates and clarifications.
- Note date and purpose when documenting approvals or handoffs.

## Packaging
- Final deliverables should include context, scope, findings, and next steps.
- Summaries should point to source artifacts and related documents.
- Broken links and stale references must be corrected before handoff.

## Review
- Check consistency in terminology, numbering, and cross-links.
- Check that required sections are present for the chosen artifact type.
- Check that the artifact can be understood without hidden context.
